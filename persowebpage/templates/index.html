After studying Physics at the University, I started my career as a physics and chemistry teacher within French secondary school. I went back to the University to study Biology and Ecology with the objective to defend a PhD and pursue my career as a research scientist.

During my PhD I focused on biological invasions. Thus my research concentrated on the role of evolutionary processes involved in the success of the invasive tree species black locust (Robinia pseudoacacia L.). I used a combined approach of population genetics and quantitative genetics to reconstruct the history of introduction from America to Europe of black locust and to detect any phenotypic differentiation on juvenile traits among opulations

I always have been very enthusiastic about teaching, thus I was involved in teaching statistics and programming with R for undergraduate and postgraduate students at Bordeaux University. I give also statistics and programming lectures to the Data Analyst and Data Scientist students at the private institute Bordeaux Data University

Recently I started also a Data Scientist career working for private companies. I focused on solving data related problem of my industrial clients as Ariane Group and Lectra. My main tasks were to collect and to analyze data with robust statistical models and to develop predictive models for anomaly detection.
